Charlottesville Police Civilian Review Board Meeting
August 12, 2021
Charlottesville Police Civilian Review Board Meeting
Charlottesville Police Civilian Review Board
municipal government does not observe local remembrances. tonight's regularly scheduled meeting of the police civilian review board starts shortly. https://t.co/g8OHg9wP25
the chair of the police civilian review board has been hinting on social media that he has some kind of bombshell news to share, i wonder if this meeting will be where he chooses to do that https://t.co/eqc78nqkMK
there's been a motion to adopt an amended agenda, screencapped here from the zoom meeting https://t.co/bJ1B5liB22
board member nancy carpenter asks for a moment of silence at the top of the meeting to recognize the anniversary today of the events of august 12, 2017. the police inaction that day served as the real impetus for police oversight in charlottesville.
the amended agenda, with nancy's friendly amendment to add a moment of silence, has been adopted.
chair bellamy brown says only 2 new complaints about police conduct have come in since the last meeting - one was about professionalism & one about failure to address a noise complaint.
next is an update about the hiring process for the executive director. chair bellamy brown recuses himself from the conversation, as he applied for the job. bill mendez says he spoke with city manager chip boyles today - there are 2 strong candidates after the interviews.
mendez says the 1st choice candidate has taken another position & is no longer in the running. the 2nd choice candidate has been sent a job offer.
@pastbriton https://t.co/9tSeTKfLQF
"it's really unclear from the public perspective" whether comments made by the public at the last meeting have been further discussed by the board or incorporated into the hearing procedures doc, says sarah burke in public comment.
some extremely valid public comments were made about that document at the last meeting! https://t.co/bCVsNCTtfx
counsel for the PCRB, cynthia hudson, says the document that was available to the public at the time of the last meeting was not the actual, correct, latest draft and actually the real draft already addressed most of the public's concerns (how could we know that?)
hudson says the draft posted on the PCRB's website AFTER the last meeting is the current draft. that document is here: https://t.co/Odn0JFGApR
nancy carpenter asks for clarification that the provision requiring complainants to submit all questions in writing ahead of a hearing has been changed - hudson confirms this is no longer a requirement.
hudson also clarifies that a particularly troubling statement in the hearing procedure doc has been edited to try to address concerns about it... the edited version is screenshot here and it really isn't better????? https://t.co/mtxFpJrKNl https://t.co/ZBE8af1bdb
they're talking about this draft like it's actually 100% better and we're all just wrong because we were looking at the wrong draft... but i'm looking at the right document now and it really isn't fully reflective of a board that heard & incorporated the community's concerns.
and as of a moment ago, it's no longer a draft. the PCRB has adopted the hearing ordinance, the july draft from their website, with one minor change (this section was amended to say ten business days) https://t.co/0NuyVemcsr
so now the board is discussing the ordinance. bill mendez tells the rest of the board that the most recent draft was emailed to them an hour ago... so i guess we are once again listening to them talk about a document we cannot see.
teresa hepler from LAJC says the provision in the ordinance that says the PCRB will investigate complaints it determines merit such an investigation leaves a lot of questions about what complaints will be investigated. the public deserves to know.
sarah burke says they should give the PCRB director & staff authority to conduct investigations instead of always outsourcing it to third parties, which will result in prohibitive costs & complaints going uninvestigated.
burke also notes that the ordinance only permits the PCRB to make disciplinary RECOMMENDATIONS only in "serious" cases - they should be able to make recommendations regardless of the severity of the complaint.
burke closes out her comments by suggesting that the most recent version of all the documents being discussed at a meeting should be included in the agenda packet so we're all looking at the same materials.
bill mendez says allowing PCRB staff to investigate complaints could create a conflict, as a city employee evaluating police conduct is not "independent," and also having in house investigation as an option could prevent spending on outside investigation when needed
mendez also says sarah's suggestion to allow the PCRB to make recommendations in any case they see fit, not just "serious" ones deserves further discussion & he's willing to delay a vote to make that change.
both nancy carpenter & jeffrey fracher say they're ready to go ahead and vote so this can go before council sooner than later. bill says he's just drafted a short resolution which he's just emailed to the chair, bellamy brown.
the lawyer, cynthia hudson, says it might be more appropriate to vote on the doc the public has seen, rather than vote on a resolution no one has had a chance to see before. (this is in response to repeated complaints from the public about them doing what bill is trying to do)
i'm honestly not 100% sure if they voted on the ordinance as it appears online or the version bill sent the board an hour before the meeting, but at least it isn't the version he wrote 30 seconds ago? whichever it was, it passed.
oh here we go, something is about to happen https://t.co/2aLSiOFvpy
PCRB chair bellamy brown says the board learned of an internal command climate survey. they received 1 of the 70 surveys and it stated "leadership takes problems personally" and is not open open to evaluation or improvement.
brown says the PCRB would also be a place for officers to bring their complaints about the working environment at CPD without fear of retaliation.
brown says the police benevolent association conducted their own survey of CPD officers and 80-90% of officers surveyed reported a negative view of the climate at CPD. brown says the PBA has been "threatened with retaliation" if they publish their findings.
damn this is just about cops hating their jobs? i thought this was gonna be something cooler.
brown reads aloud from a statement from an unnamed source at the police department, indicating that officers have "lost faith" in the leadership at the department & feel they can no longer rely on the standard procedures to address these issues.
i personally don't think it's appropriate for the police civilian review board to be hijacked as a vehicle for cops to have their workplace morale issues addressed! take it to the HR director (this is a joke because i think the city still doesn't have one)
the part about the police oversight board not being a place for cops to have their morale issues addressed was not a joke, though. the board isn't going to gain the community's trust if they prioritize making sad cops happy.
i can't believe bellamy brown spent a week vaguebooking about some bombshell news about a huge problem in the city and then derailed a meeting about police accountability to tell us the news is that cops hate their boss because she's mean.
jeff fogel says they're getting ahead of themselves - the PBA, whose internal workplace climate study they are talking about, VEHEMENTLY OPPOSED the creation of the police oversight board. what specifically are these cops upset about?
bellamy brown tried to adjourn the meeting, but it's time for one last public comment period.
"for you to jump on board with the PBA is a big mistake," fogel says, and raises questions about whether or not the board is going to be able to be objective when evaluating complaints about police conduct (which is their actual charge!)
ok, very cool and normal meeting of the *police civilian review board*. they voted to adopt an ordinance no one got to look at first and then the chair went to bat for the police benevolent society. absolutely just laughably shameful conduct here.
remember that board chair bellamy brown applied for the executive director position. the man actively working to undercut police oversight & ally the board with police is trying to get the job running the show. so help us god if they offered him this job.
