Charlottesville Parking Advisory Panel Meeting, January 19, 2021
Meeting: Charlottesville Parking Advisory Panel Meeting
Group(s): Charlottesville Parking Advisory Panel
here's the agenda for today's meeting of the parking advisory panel. chip boyles hasn't actually started as the new city manager yet, but he's visiting the meeting in his current capacity as the executive director of the thomas jefferson planning district. https://t.co/eeV4MOPAWh
2021-01-19 15:34:23 EST
chip says moving forward there needs to be an updated study on demand for parking - in the post-COVID world, demand for parking for office workers will likely be reduced (but i'm not sure how we study something as unpredictable as how people will behave when the plague passes?)
2021-01-19 15:39:13 EST
chris engel from the office of economic development says there is no intention of getting another full scale nelson nygaard parking study like we got in 2015.
2021-01-19 15:41:27 EST
city parking director rick siebert says the entire parking industry is asking this question right now - no one really knows what demand will look like after COVID. "we're a little bit away from having real numbers to determine what our needs are gonna be," chip boyles says.
2021-01-19 15:41:27 EST
"transit can be instrumental in alleviating parking issues," says garland williams, the city's director of transit. he, too, says there is a lot of uncertainty about what demand will look like after COVID.
2021-01-19 15:45:24 EST
garland williams is optimistic about a project in its early stages to better coordinate service between CAT & UTS. he also asks for a copy of the 2015 parking study to get a better idea of where the pressure points are with parking & how transit can help alleviate them.
2021-01-19 15:45:25 EST
several members of the board missed the first ten minutes of the meeting because siebert forgot to admit them from the waiting room https://t.co/boJnKnD1ox
2021-01-19 15:48:38 EST
brennen duncan, the city's traffic engineer, says they should do a study of where city employees are parking & possibly implementing more permitted residential parking for the neighborhood streets that downtown employees park on to avoid paid/timed parking downtown.
2021-01-19 15:51:02 EST
joan fenton says she suspects much of the office space will end up converted to residential - is there enough parking for that? "when the rents decrease on the commercial, all of a sudden we have apartments," she says.
2021-01-19 15:52:26 EST
siebert expresses interest in getting local data on single occupancy vehicle use (we have national & regional data, but he's looking for specifically city-level data). duncan says he's not sure how we'd get that data (the regional data is from the census).
2021-01-19 15:53:54 EST
chip boyles says VDOT has a contract with a company called streetlight that buys & aggregates data from cell phones, allowing you to see granular information about how people move around. TJPD currently has free access to some of this data.
2021-01-19 15:55:44 EST
