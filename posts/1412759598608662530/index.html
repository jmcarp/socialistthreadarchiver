Charlottesville City Council Special Meeting, July 7, 2021
Meeting: Charlottesville City Council Special Meeting
Group(s): Charlottesville City Council
Agenda Packet Minutes Recording
good morning, charlottesville city council is voting this morning to approve funding for the removal of the lee, jackson, and sacagawea statues https://t.co/nJLG5mSo1C
2021-07-07 09:05:10 EDT
"why is the sacagawea statue lumped in there?" you might be asking. in 2019, council met with descendants of sacagawea who told them the statue of their ancestor cowering was so offensive to them they were ashamed to show it to their children. https://t.co/shu5BzyGjv
2021-07-07 09:05:11 EDT
the agenda packet for this morning's meeting is here: https://t.co/2BUL0SDNYP
2021-07-07 09:06:11 EDT
vice mayor sena magill makes the motion to accept the resolution appropriating one million dollars to remove/store the jackson, lee, and sacagawea statues. it carried 5-0 without discussion.
2021-07-07 09:08:18 EDT
city manager chip boyles says the city is receiving about $19mil in ARP funding - half this year, half next year. final regulations on how it can be spent aren't out yet & his office is waiting to make final recommendations until more info is in about revenue losses. https://t.co/iiuSNKxP08
2021-07-07 09:11:55 EDT
but in the meantime, the city manager's office is recommending going ahead and funding several social & business support programs, including the peace in the streets gun violence prevention program. this can come to a vote for appropriation at the meeting on the 19th.
2021-07-07 09:13:03 EDT
boyles' office also recommends funding for eviction prevention, largely in the form of community outreach in making sure people are able to access existing eviction prevention programs, their legal rights, etc. but does include legal help for cases that do end up in court.
2021-07-07 09:15:16 EDT
on to the bus driver issue: boyles says we aren't alone in having trouble finding bus drivers, calling it a "national epidemic." his office proposes offering $2400 bonuses (to be paid over a 9 month period) and better benefits to help attract school bus drivers.
2021-07-07 09:18:21 EDT
boyles says they also recommend offering the same bonus & benefit improvements for CAT bus drivers so we don't create a problem for ourselves where CAT bus drivers just switch jobs to get the bonus, leaving us without CAT bus drivers.
2021-07-07 09:19:13 EDT
vice mayor sena magill asks if expanding the safe walk to school program would help alleviate some of the pressure to fill all the vacant bus driver positions. garland williams says the schools are working on expanding the radius of the program.
2021-07-07 09:24:20 EDT
and now on to public comment. first up is malcolm bell, an art historian and former chair of UVA's art department. he is upset that the sacagawea statue is being included in the conversation about the confederate statues. "it is completely innocent of the accusations" against it.
2021-07-07 09:26:03 EDT
sacagawea's family does not agree with this art historian. https://t.co/10K3giih62
2021-07-07 09:26:51 EDT
bell says the artist, charles keck, wanted to "honor" sacagawea. the historical context doesn't really seem to support this. https://t.co/nXyR3VSB85
2021-07-07 09:27:42 EDT
the next speaker is philip harway, calling in with an issue about the future land use map. "change is necessary and it can be good," he says, but "the city must be involved proactively." he says the consultants are planning for a 30 year timeline without a crystal ball.
2021-07-07 09:30:18 EDT
next is kimber hawkey, who doesn't think renters should get opinions (and also filed a lawsuit to stop a church from building supportive housing on their own property for people with disabilities) https://t.co/kVOb4ShAvI
2021-07-07 09:32:06 EDT
kimber accuses businesses and & developers of appropriating the rhetoric of affordable housing advocates in the interest of profit, as she appropriates the rhetoric of affordable housing advocates in the interest of her personal property values.
2021-07-07 09:33:23 EDT
kimber is offended by the "accusations of racism and NIMBYism" being thrown around in these conversations. interesting that she is able to see that those terms are related!
2021-07-07 09:34:21 EDT
lisa torres, a school board member, thanks council for their support of funding for pupil transportation. she wants to know if it's possible to go ahead & get those bus driver positions posted ASAP.
2021-07-07 09:35:27 EDT
(the positions can't officially be posted until council approves the appropriation at the meeting on the 19th, unfortunately)
2021-07-07 09:38:01 EDT
jay james from the bridge ministry has called in with another request for more city funding for the program, which provides drug treatment & post-incarceration transition services.
2021-07-07 09:38:52 EDT
john sales, the director of CRHA, has called in to thank the city manager, mayor, and others for their help with a recent situation at crescent halls.
2021-07-07 09:40:12 EDT
mayor walker reminds people listening that the decision to remove the sacagawea statue was made after meeting with her descendants and representatives of local native american groups - it isn't a response to 'protests,' as some have incorrectly said.
2021-07-07 09:42:18 EDT
i live tweeted that whole meeting back in november 2019! you can read through the comments made by both sacagawea's family & local indigenous people about their feelings on the statue https://t.co/RYEs9XUR3Z
2021-07-07 09:44:02 EDT
meeting adjourned. funding has now been allocated for the removal of the lee, jackson, and sacagawea statue, but no statement has been made about when or how the statues will be removed or where they'll go.
2021-07-07 09:44:57 EDT
