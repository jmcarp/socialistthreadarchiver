Charlottesville Human Rights Commission Meeting, July 15, 2021
Meeting: Charlottesville Human Rights Commission Meeting
Group(s): Charlottesville Human Rights Commission
Agenda Packet Recording
at tonight's human rights commission meeting, HRC chair mary bauer says she's concerned council passed the allocation of funds for legal representation for people facing eviction as a ONE TIME allocation, rather than passing an ordinance establishing it in law.
2021-07-15 18:40:54 EDT
commissioner kathryn laughon says moving toward equity focused budgeting is a valuable long term goal for the city - the commission can start thinking about what that would look like.
2021-07-15 18:41:44 EDT
"i am certain that i sent that to her," says chair mary bauer about the letter the human rights commission wrote about police violence to the police chief, which the chief claimed at the last meeting that she never received. https://t.co/Iic5jRx8LV
2021-07-15 18:48:22 EDT
regarding the last meeting, commissioner lyndele von schill says they talked a lot about budget details, but that's of less concern to her than identifying tasks/roles that can be removed from the police dept's responsibilities & reducing interaction with police overall.
2021-07-15 18:49:24 EDT
commissioner alex oxford generally agrees, but says if you shift responsibilities from police to other depts, you need to know how much of the police dept budget to shift with it - don't let police keep money they don't need & don't leave other depts overworked & underfunded
2021-07-15 18:52:25 EDT
bauer understands the reluctance to get deep into the details on the police budget, but getting the dept to account for how they spend their time actually IS critical to determining how to reallocate policing tasks to nonpolice agencies https://t.co/sBgNdzaDa5
2021-07-15 18:57:56 EDT
kathryn laughon cites national stats about ~4% of police time being spent on violent crime, but every time people ASK about how cops spend their time, the dept's social media & PR messaging shifts to nonstop talk about SHOOTINGS. it's propaganda. https://t.co/4sdu5UUAUW
2021-07-15 18:59:14 EDT
it sounds like people with different professional backgrounds are trying to answer the same question different ways. ultimately they are asking: if traffic enforcement were done by non-police personnel, how much less staff, money, and equipment would the police need?
2021-07-15 19:05:39 EDT
jessica harris is frustrated at the board's inability to connect with the police chief & build a working relationship during that confrontational meeting last month. she wants to send an olive branch to the chief.
2021-07-15 19:07:36 EDT
todd niemeier from the office of human rights says both bellamy brown & bill mendez from the police civilian review board have reached out about having a joint discussion about how the 2 groups can approach reviewing police policies.
2021-07-15 19:11:13 EDT
todd says the last round of interviews for the PCRB director were held today, so that hire should be made... soon? there's still no HR director for the city, which is complicating this whole process. there has been no human rights director since early spring 2020.
2021-07-15 19:25:18 EDT
commissioner wolfgang says the accessibility subcommittee is close to getting a proposal drafted to ask council to direct staff to create a language access plan for the city (a formal policy outlining procedures for how services can be provided for non english speakers)
2021-07-15 19:29:40 EDT
one issue facing the accessibility committee is the fact that the ADA coordinator is a position that is... facing a significant amount of turnover in recent years. the position is also housed within neighborhood development services.
2021-07-15 19:30:23 EDT
NDS, as a dept that receives some federal funding DOES have a language access plan (a requirement for federal programs). the committee wants to talk to them about how that's been used, how well it works, and how it can be expanded for all city services.
2021-07-15 19:31:27 EDT
lyndele says it's not just about talking about how they feel, but including statements about what the human rights commission is DOING to address the causes of the harm. thoughts & prayers, but also concrete actions.
2021-07-15 19:41:45 EDT
a commission member proposes drafting a letter declaring the commissions thoughts & prayers with the survivors & their families who suffered from the violence of august 12, 2017. todd says it's possible to write it as a proclamation that council can read at a meeting.
2021-07-15 19:41:45 EDT
jessica harris asks if it's possible to ask council to explore prohibiting the big confederate flags people drive around flying from the beds of their trucks. sadly, no. "people have a first amendment right to be assholes," mary bauer says wearily.
2021-07-15 19:43:59 EDT
ernest chambers praises city council & city employees for the way the statue removals were handled this weekend - everything went quickly & smoothly with minimal disruption to travel downtown.
2021-07-15 19:46:47 EDT
jeanette abi-nader thanks the activists whose work got us to the point of getting those statues down, specifically @ZyahnaB
2021-07-15 19:47:35 EDT
as far as next steps, the HRC generally agreed to reach out to the PCRB for a possible joint meeting about police policies, write a proclamation offering thoughts & prayers to people harmed on 8/12/17, and scheduled several subcommittee meetings.
