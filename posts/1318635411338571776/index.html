Charlottesville Parking Advisory Panel Meeting, October 20, 2020
Meeting: Charlottesville Parking Advisory Panel Meeting
Group(s): Charlottesville Parking Advisory Panel
it's time for the first meeting of charlottesville's parking advisory panel since before the pandemic began! what an exciting afternoon! https://t.co/MS24xVE0YS
2020-10-20 15:29:15 EDT
brand new member of the parking advisory panel @jakefmooney is on the call! i don't see @jbouie yet.
2020-10-20 15:32:03 EDT
at their work session earlier this month, council agreed to transition the parking advisory panel from a council advisory board to a staff advisory board. i'm not sure an official vote on this has actually taken place yet? https://t.co/Fnw91xMv92
2020-10-20 15:33:27 EDT
there has been a little turnover on the board, so there is currently no chair. joan fenton suggests choosing a temporary chair from the returning members then holding an election in a meeting or two for a longterm chair. kirby hutto will be temporary chair.
2020-10-20 15:34:25 EDT
rick siebert, the city's parking director, asked brian wheeler at the top of the meeting if recording the meetings was an acceptable substitute for taking minutes. it isn't. minutes are legally required for public bodies, but as a staff advisory body they wouldn't have to, right?
2020-10-20 15:35:54 EDT
introduction time - new members jakes mooney, jamelle bouie, and danny yoder introduce themselves to the other members of the panel. joan fenton introduces herself - "i own my own parking lot, so i have no problem parking, but i sympathize with all the other parking issues."
2020-10-20 15:39:29 EDT
hutto says tourists, students, and others coming downtown "for dinner and a show" are not likely to choose public transportation, so parking is necessary for the economic health of downtown.
2020-10-20 15:42:27 EDT
kirby hutto says he is concerned about the "chipping away" of parking downtown, specifically citing the loss of 50 parking spaces to the belmont bridge project. he's very excited about the new garage.
2020-10-20 15:42:27 EDT
mike rodi says although he likes to bike and walk, "we are still a community that is heavily reliant on cars." michael cusano is a relatively recent transplant, having lived here for just a year. he says he only goes downtown for "entertainment and food."
2020-10-20 15:44:00 EDT
why is everyone on the parking advisory panel originally from new york? what is going on here. does being from new york cause some genetic anxiety about parking?
2020-10-20 15:45:10 EDT
a member asks if they're going to have a public comment period, indicating that a few hands are raised... rick siebert says he doesn't think it's required (by which he means he would prefer not to hear from the public) other members of the panel seem willing to hear it
2020-10-20 15:48:39 EDT
rick says if they have to have public comment, it should be at the end so questions asked by the public don't cause redundant conversations to be had. a motion is made, seconded, and passed unanimously.
2020-10-20 15:49:37 EDT
oh, parking is free in the garages every weekend. from friday 10pm to early monday morning. this is apparently a COVID thing. i guess i haven't been downtown much in the last 7 months...
2020-10-20 15:50:40 EDT
people with monthly parking passes are being allowed to suspend their monthly garage pass during this time. (because of the wait list of over 100 people, simply terminating it would mean you can't get it back)
2020-10-20 15:53:06 EDT
willow tree moved out of downtown & worldstrides declared bankruptcy & fired a ton of people - both of these events freed up a lot of monthly parking passes. siebert hopes this will eliminate much of the waiting list.
2020-10-20 15:54:32 EDT
siebert is still pursuing the idea of an RFP to hire a company that would do parking enforcement on a contract basis. he says the chief of police is "not at all supportive" of the idea.
2020-10-20 15:57:21 EDT
we've been having this exact same conversation with no movement at all for a very long time https://t.co/M1DYCh16Wt
2020-10-20 15:58:34 EDT
siebert says the police chief's problem with the idea is that the public may not respect a civilian parking enforcement officer "and there may be assaults" AND "the police will still be blamed for the tickets"
2020-10-20 15:59:14 EDT
"no person operating a bureaucracy ever wants part of the duties of that bureaucracy taken away," siebert says, explaining why he thinks the police chief doesn't want a private company writing parking tickets.
2020-10-20 16:00:08 EDT
joan fenton says parking enforcement has been a problem (in that it isn't done at all) and when it is done, there have been issues with officers being rude. "this is something the police do not need to be doing."
2020-10-20 16:01:10 EDT
hutto agrees & supports putting out an RFP for contract parking enforcement. mike rodi says the parking enforcement personnel would need official uniforms but uniforms that are clearly not law enforcement related, making them "less threatening."
2020-10-20 16:02:40 EDT
"i agree with rick - the police chief just didn't want to give up the responsibility," joan says. but the lack of enforcement is so consistent that people just assume they won't get tickets and violate parking rules.
2020-10-20 16:02:40 EDT
these private parking enforcement personnel would not be financially incentivized in any way for the number of tickets written. they would also not be armed.
2020-10-20 16:05:43 EDT
next is discussion of the 7th street garage, which i believe is an absolutely fucking insane expense under current conditions!!! we don't need more parking and we don't have $15mil!
2020-10-20 16:11:46 EDT
as part of an agreement between the city & the county, the county will keep their courthouses in their current historic location in court square. in return, the city has to provide 90 dedicated downtown parking spaces. so... we're gonna build a whole parking garage.
2020-10-20 16:12:52 EDT
the city has hired a parking consultant to ensure the garage gets constructed, siebert says. the city will put out an RFQ for firms who can do this as a design-build project. 3-5 companies will be chosen & they'll all submit proposals.
2020-10-20 16:14:57 EDT
(remember, lucky 7 and guadalajara will be demolished for this parking garage, which is to be built across the street from the market street parking garage)
2020-10-20 16:17:09 EDT
siebert says the final garage will have 10,000ft of street front retail and 250+ parking spots. the project has to be by-right - there is concern that any need for variances to be granted will slow it down & we won't meet our obligation to the county.
2020-10-20 16:17:09 EDT
siebert says they are "having a kickoff meeting tomorrow" with the consultant to "work on finalizing a timeline." the RFQ and RFP have not been written. the obligation to the county is due fall 2023.
2020-10-20 16:18:40 EDT
if the city fails to deliver, they have to give the county back the half of the surface lot they bought from the county & the county gets to use the entire ground floor of an existing garage (missed whether he said market or water, but water would be a lot more spots)
2020-10-20 16:20:53 EDT
"part of this MOA with the county was real poison pills for the city if they fail to live up to their end of the bargain, so failure is not an option," siebert says of the agreement with the county.
2020-10-20 16:20:53 EDT
