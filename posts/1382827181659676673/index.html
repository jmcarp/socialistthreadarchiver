2021-04-15 19:19:49 EDT
"is there anything that we can say as a human rights commission about that," asks commissioner jessica harris about the police violence around the country. jeanette abi-nader expresses interest in using social media for the HRC to put out statements about issues.
2021-04-15 19:32:53 EDT
commissioner kathryn laughon says charlottesville city schools have ended their MOU with the police department & are exploring how to move forward with a police free school safety model.
2021-04-15 19:36:16 EDT
"it doesn't feel good not to say anything about it," harris says, though no one is quite certain where it falls with in the city's human rights commission's purview. (policing IS a human rights problem!)
2021-04-15 19:36:16 EDT
charlottesville city schools drew heavily from a model from toronto where the school hires more adult support staff to be in the school, but there are no police officers or security type personnel. cville schools are also hiring more social workers.
2021-04-15 19:38:17 EDT
todd says the commission can support the work of the BUCK squad (a local nonprofit that uses mediators to defuse situations that could lead to gun violence in the community, which minimizes police involvement)
2021-04-15 19:45:15 EDT
ernest chambers says the work of the BUCK squad is good but todd's suggestion that supporting their work as a way of addressing the issue of police violence isn't gonna cut it - those aren't the situations where cops are killing people.
2021-04-15 19:45:16 EDT
it's clear that everyone on the commission is shaken up by the body cam video of adam toledo's murder in chicago, which hit the internet earlier today.
2021-04-15 19:47:12 EDT
jessica harris says after seeing that video, she still has to show up to this meeting, to work, still has to be a mom to her black children. "that's trauma."
2021-04-15 19:47:13 EDT
commissioner lyndele von schill says she doesn't care what their purview is, they don't need permission. "i don't think we need to wait for permission" to issue a statement condemning the police murder of a child.
2021-04-15 19:50:03 EDT
"i certainly feel called to speak to this moment as a commission," says HRC chair mary bauer, again saying she isn't really sure what the purview of the commission is here.
2021-04-15 19:50:03 EDT
kathryn laughon says there is work being done all over in odd little pockets that the HRC can endorse, for example: the parking advisory board has talked about removing parking enforcement from the police department's duties. why are armed cops writing parking tickets?
2021-04-15 19:51:28 EDT
"if we're going to make a statement every time something like this happens," sue lewis says, "we're going to be firing off statements constantly." (it's true - cops kill people almost every day)
2021-04-15 19:56:00 EDT
lyndele says to sue's argument that they'd have to issue too many statements that it's the right thing to do. "this work is important. it needs to be done." "i don't think we need to choose when to say it and when not to say it." "we need to say it all the time."
2021-04-15 20:00:04 EDT
maybe if an official city commission issues a public statement condemning murder by police every time the police murder someone and that turns out to be a couple times a week people will realize what a horrific epidemic of state sanctioned murder we have.
2021-04-15 20:01:00 EDT
on to public comment. don gathers says any time this issue comes up, "a statement needs to be made, i don't care how frequent that is, even if it's every day twice a day. any time this issue of racism & hatred rears its ugly head," people need to speak up.
2021-04-15 20:05:56 EDT
i also made a rare public comment to thank lyndele for saying the human rights commission doesn't need permission to weigh in on police violence. cops killing people is the ultimate human rights violation - of course it's within their purview!
2021-04-15 20:07:40 EDT
jeanette tells the commission that the org where she works as her day job, cultivate charlottesville, is working with city council to make food equity part of the comprehensive plan. they'll be presenting at the may 17 meeting! and the meeting is adjourned.
