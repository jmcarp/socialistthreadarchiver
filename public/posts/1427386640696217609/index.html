dr denise bonds, the director of the blue ridge health district, is here tonight to give an update on the pandemic situation locally.
local COVID cases had completely flattened out, damn near to zero, but in the last week or two, we're starting to see new cases. dr bonds says we're seeing mainly delta variant cases. https://t.co/FTrWecXZvM
dr bonds cites the recent example of the covid outbreak in provincetown, MA - it was a highly vaxxed population, but a large public gathering still caused an outbreak. the good news is: vaccination protected people from severe illness, hospitalization, & death.
the delta variant is extremely contagious & spread is still possible among vaccinated people! get vaxxed because it DOES protect you, but it isn't the only thing you should do. masks are still recommended indoors & when distancing isn't possible https://t.co/mjQYNqTkgI
"vaccines are our way out of this pandemic," dr bonds says. get vaccinated! spread creates variants. stop the spread to stop new variants. https://t.co/FmhyBkqU6s
*regardless of vaccination status* ***EVERYONE*** entering a k-12 school in virginia must wear a mask! https://t.co/BGzLVtHVTE
honestly i expected better of charlottesville. we're vaxxed at slightly BELOW the state average. https://t.co/2L1PmuwiEY
as was announced at the BRHD press conference earlier this afternoon, immunocompromised people can now get a 3rd covid vaccine. dr bonds recommends talking to the doctor treating you for your underlying condition about whether this is right for you. https://t.co/Y09DnhnM6M
in the blue ridge health district, people in their 20s are the least likely to be vaccinated and most likely to get covid! people in their 20s ARE AT RISK from covid, regardless of their feelings of invincibility https://t.co/FqxUgl3voI

did someone sedate city council? they seem a little off.
mayor walker notes that not all masks are created equal - if they have to wear masks, why isn't there specific guidance on what KIND of mask they have to wear?
"you're always better wearing any mask than no mask," says dr bonds, saying the state likely didn't want to impose additional barriers by mandating specific kinds of masks.
mayor walker says the better masks out there are more expensive - if we are interested in using masks to reduce spread in schools, schools should be providing masks that can do that.
mayor walker also cautions against "treating the vaccine hesitant as the vaccine hostile," which she says pushes those people further away from being interested in getting the vaccine.
teletha howard, the outreach worker, says she was a little hesitant herself! she had conversations with healthcare workers & doctors to help her decide to get the vaccine. she shares her story with other people who are vaccine hesitant & talks to them without judgment.
even if you are young & otherwise healthy, if you contract covid & start to feel short of breath, you should seek medical attention, says dr bonds. it can go bad quickly, she says.
dr bonds says you can get a vaccine from the health department without any kind of documentation of any kind. providing incentives (gift cards, etc) would require that to change, which is why she's hesitant to do that.
i've developed a strange sort of parasocial relationship with the employees of my local health department. i trust them and i hope they are happy and healthy.
wow huge news in the announcements section: the city of charlottesville got an award for achievement of excellence in financial reporting. very exciting stuff! https://t.co/hJZSGrG9T8
no takers for public comment on consent agenda items. lloyd snook has some individual line edits for item 4 (a simple ctrl+F for words like 'man' doesn't neatly solve the problem, things like "man made" shouldn't become "individual made" etc) https://t.co/DCzVgW8oXm
mayor walker asks if there's a reason why the nonprofit in question is not named in item 3 & if the tax mistake is the business' fault, does the city still have to pay them back? todd divers says there are rules about divulging taxpayer information.
divers says he did put the business' name on the documentation last time this happened, "and i probably shouldn't have" because giving specific figures about taxes paid verges on divulging protected taxpayer info (i assume he means the silverchair refund) https://t.co/iwxOXIAqmB
again, as with the silverchair situation: regardless of whose mistake it was, the city has to pay interest on overpayment of business license tax. "it's dumb," divers concedes, but it is the law.
the consent agenda carries 5-0. for city manager's response to matters by the public, city manager chip boyles says he has nothing new to report. on to public comment - 4 people have pre-registered, leaving 12 open spots.
marta keane, the CEO at the jefferson area board for aging, reads some figured from their recent survey - she's referring to a report i can't see, but it sounds like both seniors & volunteers seem satisfied.
deonte johnson says the city provides only 1 early voting site, which is downtown. placing another early voting site near UVA grounds would increase accessibility for students to vote.
this is a good idea and the city should do it. i waited in line for HOURS to vote early at the downtown early voting location. tens of thousands of UVA students and employees would probably love having a more convenient place to vote early in person.
vice mayor sena magill raises the issue that UVA dorms are in the county, so wouldn't the county be the one to set this up? (it's true that all the dorms & most UVA buildings are in the county, but most UVA students living off grounds are city residents)
chair of the human rights commission, mary bauer, reads a statement from the commission on the anniversary of august 12, 2017 & reaffirms their commitment to equity & racial justice.
(speaking of human rights - the captions & transcript functions that zoom has are turned off for this particular meeting! i had really been enjoying those accessibility features)
mary pettis has been driving a bus in charlottesville for 35 years. she called in tonight to ask council to allow bus drivers to unionize.
"i love driving, i love my passengers, i just need more from it financially," pettis says, urging council to allow transit operators to unionize.
"i personally had to move from charlottesville to waynesboro because i couldn't afford to live in charlottesville. i have 3 jobs because i don't make enough money just driving a bus," pettis says.
"it shouldn't take collective bargaining," mayor walker says. walker says she "fully supports" it, but it shouldn't be necessary, that the city should be meeting these needs already.
marcia geyer says the city manager should work with the school board to determine how many masks are needed in the schools & charitable donations of $ and masks should be collected & provided to students.
tanesha hudson called from what sounded like a party or a restaurant to say in-person meetings should resume. earlier in the summer, the city had been aiming for september for this, but it has obviously been pushed back due to widespread community transmission of COVID.
charles neer is still very concerned about parking at the proposed 8 unit apartment building at 1206 carlton https://t.co/vkvp6NlHPX
ms katrina turner has called in to discuss the alleged morale problems within the police department. she asks if the city will believe there are problems with the leadership at CPD now that police are saying it, when community members have been saying it. https://t.co/2iyScv0cRH
mayor walker says the community is "easily manipulated" and "this is an example of that." she does not elaborate on what this means.
greg wright of the fire dept has called in to voice support for collective bargaining for all city employees. he agrees that "it should not have to come to this" but all city employees deserve to have a seat at the table.
ok, that concludes public comment. we'll recess for 15 minutes & then get on to the rest of the agenda https://t.co/hghelVml8x

lloyd snook moves to approve the SUP, payne seconds. mayor walker again votes no, with heather hill joining her in voting no this time, but the special use permit for the now-rezoned 1206 carlton ave passes 3-2.
on to the final agenda item - collective bargaining for city employees! https://t.co/5cIGcutp6N
city manager chip boyles cites the example of other localities in VA who are embarking on complicated journeys (involving outside consultants and long timelines) toward collective bargaining
boyles says the city doesn't at this time have sufficient information to make a good decision on a collective bargaining ordinance & council should reject the ordinance put forward on behalf of the firefighters. https://t.co/ApJfYyr4tu
boyles' recommendation to council is to vote down the firefighters' proposal but pass a resolution asking him (the city manager) to develop a collective bargaining ordinance for the city.
vice mayor sena magill voices support for the idea of collective bargaining. lloyd snook says he wants to make sure people understand that loudoun & alexandria got started on this "almost right after the statute was passed last year... and we did not."
snook adds that the city didn't even have a city attorney at the time this law changed last year. he wants greg wright & the fire dept to know their letter isn't being ignored & hopes the city will be able to move forward "with reasonable dispatch"
payne agrees that it's the correct decision to take the time to get this right, but cautions against letting it get delayed past the current budget cycle or let it languish indefinitely.
lol i forgot the city still doesn't have an HR director. that does complicate a wide variety of things!
council voted unanimously voted to not approve the collective bargaining ordinance put forward on behalf of the fire dept and unanimously in favor of having the city manager develop a collective bargaining ordinance for the city.
boyles says the posting for the HR director closed on friday, so they will be able to start trying to hire an applicant. with no other business, we are on to closing matters by the public... and with no takers... we are adjourned in under 3 hours!
